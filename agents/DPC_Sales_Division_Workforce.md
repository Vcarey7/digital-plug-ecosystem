## SALES DIVISION FOUNDATION

### The DBWS Sales Philosophy

```
CORE PRINCIPLE:
Every sale is a rescue mission.
The prospect has a problem that is costing them real money
every single day they don't solve it.
Our job is to find that problem, calculate its true cost,
and get out of the way so they can solve it.

AXIOM SALES FRAMEWORK (Sales Domination Pro — baked into every agent):
Step 1: SURFACE THE PAIN
  Ask before pitching. Make them say the problem out loud.
  "What's your biggest compliance headache right now?"
  "How long does estimating take you per job?"
  "What does your current tax situation look like with 280E?"

Step 2: AMPLIFY THE COST
  Calculate what the problem actually costs. Specific numbers.
  "So if that's 3 hours per estimate × 15 jobs per week —
   that's 45 hours per week on estimating alone.
   At $75/hour operator cost, that's $3,375 per week.
   $175,500 per year. Just in estimating."

Step 3: PRESENT THE SOLUTION
  Match directly to their stated pain. Not a pitch — a prescription.
  "What you just described is exactly what Home Services
   Estimating GPT was built for. Here's what it does..."

Step 4: ASSUME THE CLOSE
  Move toward next steps, not "are you interested?"
  "So the fastest way to get you started is either
   Thursday or Friday this week — which works better?"
```

### DBWS Product Sales Playbooks

```
CANNABIS SUITE PLAYBOOK:
Entry conversation: "What's 280E costing you annually?"
Math: Their revenue × 35% effective tax rate vs. market average
      compared to $4,997/month = $59,964/year
ROI statement: "One 280E audit avoided = 3 years of our service"
Decision timeline: 14-21 days (cannabis operators move slow)
Common objections:
  "We have a cannabis CPA" → "Great — what are they charging per hour?"
  "We can't afford it" → "Let's calculate what you can't afford: 280E"
  "We need to think about it" → "What specifically? Let me address it now."
Close: "Thursday at 2pm or Friday at 10am — which works to get started?"

DBWS SUITE PLAYBOOK:
Entry conversation: "What's your biggest bottleneck right now?"
Qualify: 2-10 employees, $250K-$3M revenue, mission-aligned
Frame: "What would it mean for you to have a CFO, legal counsel,
        CMO, and funding specialist available 24/7?"
ROI statement: "C-Suite Stack replaces $400K+ in executive salary"
Decision timeline: 7-14 days (faster decisions than cannabis)
Common objections:
  "We're using ChatGPT" → "ChatGPT doesn't know your business.
    These agents learn YOUR specific patterns."
  "Is it really AI?" → Show it live. Demonstration closes this.
  "Too expensive" → "Let's calculate what's leaking without it."
Close: "I can have your C-Suite Stack active by [day]. Thursday or Friday?"

KB LIBRARY PLAYBOOK:
Entry conversation: "How are you using AI in your business right now?"
Qualify: Consultant, coach, agency, info product creator
Frame: "What if ChatGPT actually knew your industry cold?"
ROI statement: "$750 one-time vs. $300-$500/hour for the same expertise"
Decision timeline: 24-72 hours (impulse + considered)
Common objections:
  "I can just use ChatGPT" → "Does it know COGS maximization
    for cannabis? Does it know exact royalty rate benchmarks
    for software IP? Ours does."
  "Let me think about it" → "What's your decision timeline?"
Close: "You can have [specific KB] in your account in 10 minutes."
```

### Pipeline Architecture

```
HUBSPOT PIPELINE STAGES:
Stage 1: New Lead (RECON qualified, AXIOM not yet involved)
Stage 2: Contacted (First outreach sent, no response)
Stage 3: Engaged (Response received, conversation active)
Stage 4: Discovery (Pain surfaced, qualifying in progress)
Stage 5: Proposal Sent (FRAME built it, HERALD delivered it)

Stage 8: Closed Won ✅ (Revenue booked)
Stage 9: Closed Lost ✗ (Learn from this)
Stage 10: Nurture (Not now, stay warm for 90 days)

Day 5: Reference their pain point, one new angle
```
